# Q1 2026 Hyperscaler AI Capex Check

**As of:** 2026-05-24  **Sources fetched:** 12

## TL;DR

- **Aggregate Q1 2026 capex** for the four hyperscalers was **~$130.6 billion**, up 72% year-over-year. Amazon led at $43.2B, followed by Alphabet ($35.7B), Microsoft ($31.9B), and Meta ($19.8B) (Platformonomics scoreboard; company 10-Qs/earnings releases).
- **Full-year 2026 guidance** now sums to **~$695–725 billion** at the midpoint, up from roughly **$660 billion** in prior consensus estimates. Microsoft raised its calendar-year guide to ~$190B (from ~$155B Street), Meta lifted its range to $125–145B (from $115–135B), Alphabet edged up to $180–190B (from $175–185B), and Amazon held at ~$200B (Platformonomics; Om Malik blog; Goldman Sachs research via Finviz/Finexus).
- **Goldman Sachs** pegs Wall Street consensus for hyperscaler capex at **$667 billion** for 2026, up $127 billion since the start of the Q4 2025 earnings season and implying 62% YoY growth (Goldman Sachs via Finviz).
- **AI/data-center share** is explicitly disclosed by Microsoft (~two-thirds of capex to short-lived assets, primarily GPUs/CPUs) and Alphabet (~60% of technical infrastructure in servers, 40% in data centers/networking). Amazon and Meta cite AWS/gen-AI and servers/data centers, respectively, without precise percentages.
- **Tone divergence:** Amazon and Microsoft stress monetization visibility—Microsoft says it has "high confidence" the spend will be monetized; Amazon notes early-years FCF is "challenged" but cumulatively attractive. Alphabet admits it is "compute constrained" and sees 2027 capex rising "significantly." Meta raised guidance largely on component pricing but acknowledged memory cost headwinds and announced workforce reductions to offset infrastructure spend.

### 2. Full-Year 2026 Capex Guidance vs. Prior Expectations

| Company | Prior Guidance/Consensus | Updated Guidance (Apr 2026) | Change |
|---------|------------------------|----------------------------|--------|
| Microsoft | ~$155B (consensus) | **~$190B** | **+$35B** |
| Alphabet | $175–185B | **$180–190B** | **+$5B** (both ends) |
| Amazon | ~$200B | **~$200B** | **Unchanged** |
| Meta | $115–135B | **$125–145B** | **+$10B** (both ends) |
| **Combined (midpoint)** | **~$660B** | **~$710B** | **+$50B** |

**Microsoft:** CFO Amy Hood stated, "For calendar year 2026, we expect to invest roughly $190 billion in capital expenditures which includes approximately $25 billion from the impact of higher component pricing" (Motley Fool transcript). Q4 FY26 capex is expected to exceed $40B, up from $31.9B in Q3.

**Alphabet:** CFO Anat Ashkenazi said the board updated full-year 2026 CapEx guidance to **$180–190 billion**, up from the prior $175–185 billion, "to account for the Intersect acquisition completed in March" (Motley Fool transcript). She also flagged that 2027 capex should "significantly increase compared to 2026."

**Amazon:** Maintained its ~$200B forecast (from February 2026). CFO Brian Olsavsky noted that rapid CapEx increases in high-growth periods mean "the early years' free cash flow" is challenged until capacity is monetized (Motley Fool transcript).

**Meta:** CFO Susan Li raised 2026 capex guidance to **$125–145 billion**, up from $115–135 billion, "mainly due to elevated component pricing and increased data center costs" (Motley Fool transcript). CEO Mark Zuckerberg added that "most of that is due to higher component costs, particularly memory pricing."

### 4. Street Consensus Comparison

- **Goldman Sachs** (via Finviz, Futunn, Finexus): Wall Street consensus now projects hyperscaler capex of **$667 billion** in 2026, up **$127 billion** since the start of Q4 2025 earnings season and implying **62% YoY growth** (Goldman Sachs research, cited by Finviz, 2026-05).
- **Platformonomics** scorecard: Prior aggregate guidance was ~$640B+; post-earnings aggregate is **$695–725 billion** (Platformonomics, 2026-04-30).
- **Om Malik synthesis**: The four companies collectively told investors they will spend roughly **$700 billion** in 2026, nearly double 2025 levels (Om.co, 2026-04-30).

**Verdict:** The four hyperscalers are collectively spending **more** than the Street expected coming into the quarter. The consensus has been revised upward by roughly $50B at the midpoint.

### 5. Divergence in Tone on AI Demand Durability vs. Capex Digestion

| Company | Tone on Demand | Tone on Capex Digestion / FCF |
|---------|---------------|------------------------------|
| **Microsoft** | Bullish. AI business ARR surpassed $37B (+123%). Azure capacity "supply expected to remain constrained at least through 2026." | Confident. "High confidence this will be monetized well... we already have customer commitments for a substantial portion of it." But warned $25B of $190B is pure component price inflation. |
| **Alphabet** | Very bullish. Cloud backlog nearly doubled QoQ to $462B. Pichai: "We are compute constrained in the near term." | Accepting of near-term pressure. Ashkenazi warned of "higher depreciation expense and related data center operations costs." 2027 capex will "significantly increase." |
| **Amazon** | Bullish. AWS revenue accelerated to 28%; Trainium commitments exceed $225B. | Cautious on near-term FCF. Jassy: "In times of very high growth... the early years' free cash flow is challenged until these initial tranches of capacity are being monetized." |
| **Meta** | Bullish on product, but cost-driven. Zuckerberg: "Every sign... gives us confidence in this investment." But raised guidance mostly on component costs. | Most cautious. Raised capex on memory pricing, announced workforce reductions in May "to offset infrastructure investments." FCF of $12.4B in Q1 vs. $125–145B annual capex guide. |

**Key divergence:** Microsoft and Amazon emphasize *monetization visibility* and customer commitments as justification for the spend. Alphabet is transparent about being *supply-constrained* and sees the constraint extending into 2027. Meta raised its guide primarily because of *input cost inflation* (memory) rather than demand upside, and is cutting headcount to help digest the spend.
